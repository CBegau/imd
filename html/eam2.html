Implementation of EAM2

The Embedded Atom Method (EAM) was implemented by Erik Bitzek. It is more general than the eam implementation because it is not limited to potentials of the Finnis-Sinclair type.

  • Basic Theory

    The Embedded Atom Method was suggested by Daw and Baskes (M. S. Daw and M. I. Baskes, Phys. Rev. B 29, 6443 (1984); S. M. Foiles, M. I. Baskes, and M. S. Daw, Phys. Rev. B 33, 1986) as a way to overcome the main problem with two-body potentials: the coordination independance of the bond strenght, while still being acceptable fast (about 2 times slower than pair potentials).

    Ideas from the Density Functional Theory or the Tight Binding formalism may lead to the following form for the total energy:

    Etot = \sumi,jN \Phiij(rij) + \sumiN F(\rhohi)
    \rhohi = \sumj \Rhoatij(rij)

    The pair potential term \Phiij(rij) represents the electrostatic core-core repulsion, the cohesive term F(\rhohi) represents the energy the ion core gets when it is "embedded" in the local electron density \rhohi. This local electron density is constructed as a superposition of contributions \Rhoatij(rij) from neighboring atoms.

    Also belonging to this analytical form are models like the glue model, and the Finnis-Sinclair potentials. See also the Teeseminar about EAM.

  • Use of eam2

    The eam2 option works on risc and mpi plattforms. It now uses actio=reaction between the atoms on the same processor. eam2 should work with most of the common options. See Tests for options that are guaranteed to work.

    To use eam2 one needs tables of three functions. Therefore the parameter file should contain lines indicating these tables, e.g.:

    core_potential_file Potentials/Ni_u3/Phi_r2.Ni_u3.dat
    embedding_energy_file Potentials/Ni_u3/F_rho.Ni_u3.dat
    atomic_e-density_file Potentials/Ni_u3/Rho_r2.Ni_u3.dat

    To allow different stepwidths and starting/ending points for the potentials, the tables have the following format:

    core_potential_file, atomic_e-density_file:
    n*n lines with:
    r_begin r_end dr
    followed by n*n blocks of function values (equidistant in r^2)
    for the atom pairs ij: 00, 01,... 10, 11,...

    It is the responsibility of the creator of the potential to make sure, that the functions of r go smoothly to zero and that the last four values are 0.0. If you're having an exotic core potential with ij != ji, you have to use the option asympot.

    embedding_energy_file:
    n lines with:
    rho_begin rho_end drho
    followed by n blocks of values equidistant in rho

  • Existing Potentials

    Potentials with documentation and sample structures, parameter files and tools can be found in ~imd/imd-samples/eam2. At the moment there are three potentials for Nickel and one for NiAl.

  • Tests

    The eam2 option was tested by

    • comparing the energy output for a dynamical nve simulation with those produced by an other MD program (FEAT by Peter Gumbsch)
    • comparing the simulated values for the cohesive energy, vacancy formation energy, surface energy, bulkmodulus, lattice constant, surface relaxation,... for different potentials with published data for the specific potentials and experimental data
    • checking the conservation of energy at different temperatures

    Therefore one may conclude that the eam2 option leads to correct results at least for: one and two different atom types, with and without periodic boundary conditions, on risc and mpi plattforms, with the MIK, NVE, NVT options.
