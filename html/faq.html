3.
(Dieter Wolfangel) Units: IMD uses no unit system by itself. What counts are the units in which the potential has been computed.

Particularly, IMD does not use reduced units. But if the potential tabales are computed from standard Lennard-Jones interactions, it seems as if reduced units are applied.

It is suitable to scale the values of mass, atom distance and energy in such a way that there absolut value is of the order 1. For example the mass in Dekau (10 atomic units), the atom distance in Ångstrøm (10 nm), and the energy in mRy (13.56 meV).

For aluminium (1.66-26kg) one gets a factor mass1/2*length/energy1/2 equal to 2.7-13 for the rescaling of the time unit 1 into seconds.

Thus the upper limit for a time step should be 0.01 (fs), if the phonons with the highest frequency shall still be resolved (Debye temperature 400K, equivalent to 8.312 Hz or 1.2-13 s).

The IMD temperature 1 is equivalent to 157 Kelvin (energy/kB).

QUESTION : I have changed the potential table, and the program gives me strange results.

ANSWER : The problem can be the potential table itself. IMD needs a potential table in which the values of the potential are computed in points EQUALLY displaced in r2, and not in r.

QUESTION : I have used the displacement field of a crack, and I have scaled it to obtain a new start configuration relative to scaled boundary conditions as well, but the energies after very few time steps diverges.

ANSWER : The problem is that the displacement field is ill-conditioned in the neighborhood of the crack tip. The possible solutions are two:

1) You use a smaller time step, but this solution is not recommended, for you're using a different dynamic induced by potologic initial conditions.

2) You have to reach you displacement field step-by-step. Just scale your initial configuration of a smaller value. Then let it relaxe for very few time step, and rescale the output configuration of a little values again, and so on till you have reached the desired value of displacement during relaxation of intermediate configurations. The final configuration will be the right initial configuration of your problem.

7.
IMD uses the full minimal image condition, i.e. each atom is placed back into the original sample. This may give wrong results in diffusion simulations.
